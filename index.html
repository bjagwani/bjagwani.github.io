About Me!

Out of the teaching realm into the Analytics & Management world

The number one thing that has influenced all my decisions throughout my career is DATA. Whether it was analyzing data on diabetes for my Master’s thesis or planning student study schedules based on their performance. My professional life could be summed up in 3 phases:

Phase 1: Master's degree in Microbiology and starting as a faculty member
After completing my Master's degree, I started working as a Faculty member, which has helped me become an active listener and an excellent communicator. In this role, I had to come up with a course schedule, incorporate various teaching methodologies, present the schedule to my superiors and executives & make sure to deliver within the timeline. Because I had to prepare and give presentations not only to the students but also to my peers and superiors, I developed excellent presentation skills. 

Phase 2: Introduced to Analytics & Management
At the end of 2018, the Institute I worked with dissolved for a few months. That gave me some time to think about what I wanted to do going forward in my career. My dad shared an article titled ‘The power of data analytics!’ which piqued my interested, and I started by watching a simple 'Excel Data Analysis' video on YouTube. That's where it all started! I started using Excel not only for organizing all the research about different careers that I could pursue but also for planning what all courses that I needed to take and tracking my schedule.I started applying to internships and received an amazing opportunity to work as a Business Analyst Intern where I gathered knowledge about the Business side of things and got to work on real-world data.

Phase 3: Master's degree in Information Systems
When I came to the US, I knew I wanted to study further in the analytics/ management space. I am now a graduate student at Santa Clara University, doing a Masters in Information Systems and have learnt so much in such a short period. Here, I have led multiple projects, was appointed as teaching assistant for Java course (never realized I was good at coding) and was project manager (Research Assistant) for a brief amount of time. I am now working as a Practicum Student for VMware where I am the liaison representing our team to VMware, and have exciting work that involves figuring out root-cause analysis of AWS price spikes using Python and Machine Learning. 

When I am not learning new skills, you will find me reading, baking or traveling!
Feel free to message me! Looking forward to connecting with you!
